# 1220. Registration Categories

## (a) Definition of Principal and Principal Registration Categories

### (1) Definition of Principal
A "principal" is any person associated with a member who is actively engaged in managing the member's investment banking or securities business (supervision, solicitation, conduct of business, training), including sole proprietors, officers, partners, OSJ managers, directors, CEO, CFO, and any person performing principal-required functions — "actively engaged in management" includes implementing corporate policies, managerial decision-making authority, and management-level supervisory responsibilities.

### (2) General Securities Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each principal must register as a General Securities Principal, except: (i) those performing Compliance Officer, FINOP, Principal Financial/Operations Officer, Investment Banking Principal, Research Principal, Securities Trader Principal, or Registered Options Principal functions must register in those categories; (ii) those limited to Government Securities, Investment Company/Variable Contracts, DPP, or Private Securities Offerings Principal functions may register in those categories instead; (iii) those limited to General Securities Sales Supervisor functions may register in that category instead (with options registration still required for options sales); and (iv) those limited to Supervisory Analyst functions may register in that category instead (with Research Principal registration required if approving equity research content).

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Corporate Securities Representative and General Securities Principal on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered for limited corporate securities supervisory activities; all others must register as a General Securities Representative and either pass the General Securities Principal exam or register as a General Securities Sales Supervisor and pass the Sales Supervisor Module exam.

### (3) Compliance Officer

#### (A) Requirement
Each Chief Compliance Officer designated on Schedule A of Form BD per Rule 3130(a) must register as a Compliance Officer, subject to the exception in (a)(3)(C).

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as General Securities Representative and General Securities Principal (or as Compliance Official in CRD) on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must either register as a General Securities Representative and pass the General Securities Principal exam, or pass the Compliance Official exam.

#### (C) Exception
A CCO of a member with limited investment banking or securities business may register in a principal category corresponding to the limited scope of the member's business.

### (4) Financial and Operations Principal and Introducing Broker-Dealer Financial and Operations Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Members operating under SEA Rules 15c3-1(a)(1)(ii), (a)(2)(i), or (a)(8) must designate a FINOP; other members subject to SEA Rule 15c3-1 must designate either a FINOP or Introducing BD FINOP — responsible for final approval/preparation of financial reports, supervision of report preparers and books/records maintainers, financial responsibility rule compliance, back office operations oversight, and all other financial/operational management matters.

#### (B) Designation of Principal Financial Officer and Principal Operations Officer
Each member must designate a Principal Financial Officer (financial filings/records) and Principal Operations Officer (day-to-day operations including securities/funds receipt/delivery, asset safeguarding, margin, dividend/reorganization processing); self-clearing members must designate separate persons for each role (waiver available under Rule 9600 for limited-size firms); introducing members may designate one person for all three roles; each designated PFO/POO must register as FINOP or Introducing BD FINOP.

#### (C) Qualifications
FINOP candidates must pass the FINOP exam; Introducing BD FINOP candidates must pass either the FINOP exam or the Introducing BD FINOP exam.

### (5) Investment Banking Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each principal supervising investment banking activities specified in paragraph (b)(5) must register as an Investment Banking Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Investment Banking Representative and General Securities Principal on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must register as an Investment Banking Representative and pass the General Securities Principal exam.

### (6) Research Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each principal responsible for approving equity research report content or supervising equity Research Analysts/Supervisory Analysts must register as a Research Principal, except: (i) those limited to approving equity research content may register as Supervisory Analyst instead; (ii) those limited to equity research disclosure compliance review may register as General Securities Principal instead; and (iii) those limited to approving debt or third-party research may register as General Securities Principal or Supervisory Analyst instead.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Research Principal on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must register as either a Research Analyst or Supervisory Analyst and pass the General Securities Principal exam.

### (7) Securities Trader Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each principal supervising securities trading activities specified in paragraph (b)(4) must register as a Securities Trader Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Candidates must register as a Securities Trader and pass the General Securities Principal exam.

### (8) Registered Options Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each member transacting in options with the public must have at least one Registered Options Principal; each principal supervising options sales practices (including Rule 3110(a)(2) designees) must register as ROP, except those limited to activities supervisable by a General Securities Sales Supervisor under Rule 2360 may register in that category instead.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as ROP on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must register as a General Securities Representative and pass the ROP exam.

### (9) Government Securities Principal

#### (A) Requirement
Each principal whose activities include managing/supervising the member's government securities business (underwriting, trading, sales, financial advisory, research/investment advice, or public investor communications related thereto) or supervising government securities processing/clearance or related recordkeeping must register as a Government Securities Principal — alternatively, they may register as a General Securities Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Government Securities Principal on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must register as a General Securities Representative.

### (10) General Securities Sales Supervisor

#### (A) Principals Engaged in Limited Activities
Principals whose supervisory responsibilities are limited to securities sales activities (account approval, sales training, branch-level recordkeeping) may register as a General Securities Sales Supervisor, but may not supervise underwriting origination/structuring, market-making commitments, 15c3-3 custody, or overall financial responsibility compliance.

#### (B) Qualifications
Candidates must register as a General Securities Representative and pass the General Securities Sales Supervisor exams.

### (11) Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Principal

#### (A) Principals Engaged in Limited Activities
Principals whose activities are limited to those specified in paragraph (b)(7) may register as an Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Candidates must register as either a General Securities Representative or an Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Representative and pass the Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Principal exam.

### (12) Direct Participation Programs Principal

#### (A) Principals Engaged in Limited Activities
Principals whose activities are limited to those specified in paragraph (b)(8) may register as a Direct Participation Program Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Candidates must register as either a General Securities Representative or a Direct Participation Programs Representative and pass the DPP Principal exam.

### (13) Private Securities Offerings Principal

#### (A) Principals Engaged in Limited Activities
Principals whose activities are limited to those specified in paragraph (b)(9) may register as a Private Securities Offerings Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Private Securities Offerings Representative and General Securities Principal on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must register as a Private Securities Offerings Representative and pass the General Securities Principal exam.

### (14) Supervisory Analyst

#### (A) Principals Engaged in Limited Activities
Principals may register as Supervisory Analyst if limited to approving: equity research report content, debt research report content, third-party research reports, retail communications under Rule 2241(a)(11)(A), or other non-research-report research communications (with product expertise) — equity research Supervisory Analysts must be supervised by a Research Principal.

#### (B) Qualifications
Candidates must pass the Supervisory Analyst exam; FINRA will waive the securities analysis portion (Part II) upon verification of passing CFA Level I under the Rule 9600 Series.

## (b) Definition of Representative and Representative Registration Categories

### (1) Definition of Representative
A "representative" is any person associated with a member (including assistant officers other than principals) who is engaged in the member's investment banking or securities business, including supervision, solicitation, conduct of business, or training.

### (2) General Securities Representative

#### (A) Requirement
Each representative must register as a General Securities Representative, except: (i) those performing Operations Professional, Securities Trader, Investment Banking Representative, or Research Analyst functions must register in those categories; and (ii) those limited to Investment Company/Variable Contracts, DPP, or Private Securities Offerings Representative functions may register in those categories instead.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as General Securities Representative on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and General Securities Representative exam.

### (3) Operations Professional

#### (A) Requirement

##### (i) Covered Persons
Senior management with direct responsibility over covered functions, persons designated by such senior management as supervisors/managers/approvers of work in furtherance of covered functions, and persons with authority to materially commit a member's capital or bind the member to material contracts in furtherance of covered functions must register as Operations Professionals.

##### (ii) Covered Functions
Client on-boarding, funds collection/maintenance/sweeps/disbursement, securities/funds receipt/delivery/account transfers, bank/custody/depository account management/reconciliation, settlement/fail control/buy-ins/segregation/possession/control, trade confirmation/account statements, margin, stock loan/securities lending, prime brokerage, pricing model approval for valuations, financial control (general ledger/treasury), financial regulatory report preparation, business requirements for trading/covered-function systems and their validation, business security/IT requirements and policies, information entitlement policies, and books/records posting for integrity and compliance.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered in numerous existing representative/principal categories on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered, as are persons who register in those categories after that date; all others must pass the SIE and Operations Professional exam — FINRA may accept alternative domestic/foreign qualifications, and registrants have a 120-day grace period to pass applicable exams while functioning as an Operations Professional.

### (4) Securities Trader

#### (A) Requirement
Representatives engaged in proprietary trading, agency execution, or direct supervision of such activities in equity/preferred/convertible debt securities effected OTC must register as Securities Traders (excluding those trading principally for affiliated registered investment companies); persons primarily responsible for designing/developing/significantly modifying algorithmic trading strategies (automated order generation/routing systems, excluding pure order-routing systems) or supervising such activities must also register.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Securities Trader on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and Securities Trader exam.

### (5) Investment Banking Representative

#### (A) Requirement
Representatives whose activities involve advising on or facilitating debt/equity offerings (origination, underwriting, marketing, structuring, syndication, pricing, allocation, stabilization) through private placement or public offering, or advising on/facilitating M&A, tender offers, restructurings, asset sales, divestitures, reorganizations, or business combinations (including fairness/solvency opinions) must register as Investment Banking Representatives.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Investment Banking Representative on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and Investment Banking Representative exam.

#### (C) Exceptions

##### (i) New Employee Training Program
Associates in a member's training program are exempt from registration for up to six months from first engaging in IB activities (max two years in the program), provided the member maintains records of the program details and participant activities.

##### (ii) Limited Activities
Registration is not required if activities are limited solely to DPP securities placement, private securities offerings as specified in (b)(9), or retail/institutional sales and trading.

### (6) Research Analyst

#### (A) Requirement
Each person functioning as a research analyst (primary job function is investment research, primarily responsible for equity research report substance or named on an equity report per Rule 2241) must register, except non-member foreign affiliate employees residing outside the U.S. who contribute to globally branded/foreign affiliate reports but not to member-specific or non-globally-branded research — subject to conditions including pre-use review by a Research Principal or Supervisory Analyst, Rule 2241 compliance, prominent disclosure of contributing affiliates and unregistered foreign analysts, front-page or hyperlinked disclosures, and recordkeeping evidencing the exemption and Rule 2241 compliance.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered as Research Analyst on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and Research Analyst exams — FINRA will waive the analytical portion (Series 86) for CFA Level I and II passers (or CMT Level I and II for technical analysts), provided continuous service or registration within two years; Series 87 regulatory portion must still be passed.

### (7) Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Representative

#### (A) Representatives Engaged in Limited Activities
Representatives whose activities are limited to solicitation/purchase/sale of redeemable investment company securities, original-distribution closed-end fund securities, variable contracts/insurance premium funding programs (except Section 3(a)(8) exempt contracts), or municipal fund securities (MSRB Rule D-12) may register in this category.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and the Investment Company and Variable Contracts Products Representative exam.

### (8) Direct Participation Programs Representative

#### (A) Representatives Engaged in Limited Activities
Representatives whose activities are limited to solicitation/purchase/sale of DPP equity interests or debt may register in this category — "direct participation programs" means flow-through tax consequence programs (oil/gas, cattle, condominium securities, Subchapter S offerings, etc.), excluding REITs, qualified pension/profit-sharing/IRA/403(b) plans, registered investment companies, and nationally exchange-listed programs.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and DPP Representative exam.

### (9) Private Securities Offerings Representative

#### (A) Representatives Engaged in Limited Activities
Representatives whose activities are limited to effecting primary offering sales not involving a public offering under Securities Act Sections 3(b), 4(2), or 4(6) may register in this category, provided they do not effect sales of municipal/government securities or DPP interests.

#### (B) Qualifications
Persons registered on/within two years before October 1, 2018 are grandfathered; all others must pass the SIE and Private Securities Offerings Representative exam — FINRA may also deem qualified any person who effected private securities offerings while employed by a bank during May 12 to November 12, 1999.

## Supplementary Material

### .01 Foreign Registrations
Persons in good standing with the UK Financial Conduct Authority or a Canadian stock exchange/securities regulator are exempt from the SIE requirement.

### .02 Security Futures Activities
Persons registered as General Securities Representative, UK/Canada Securities Representative, Options Representative, ROP, or General Securities Sales Supervisor may engage in security futures activities after completing a Firm Element program (Rule 1240) addressing security futures products.

### .03 Members With One Registered Options Principal
Members must promptly notify FINRA if their sole ROP is terminated, resigns, or becomes incapacitated, agree in writing to cease options activities requiring ROP approval, and cease options business entirely if a new ROP is not qualified within two weeks (or the earliest exam date), though closing transactions to reduce existing positions are permitted.

### .04 Scope of General Securities Sales Supervisor Registration Category
The GSSS category is an alternate principal registration allowing qualification to supervise sales of all securities (corporate, municipal, options, investment company, variable contracts, DPP, and security futures) through one registration, reducing the burden of separate SRO qualifications — registrants may also qualify in other principal categories, and it applies to branch managers, regional/national sales managers, and other persons supervising only sales activities.

### .05 Scope of Operations Professional Requirement
Persons limited to ancillary, supportive, or advisory roles (e.g., internal audit, legal, compliance reviewers without primary responsibility) or solely clerical/ministerial activities in covered functions are not required to register as Operations Professionals; "material" for capital/contract commitment is based on pre-established spending guidelines and risk policies; foreign broker-dealer employees limited to facilitating clearance/settlement of foreign securities transactions on a DVP/RVP basis for member customers are also exempt.

### .06 Eliminated Registration Categories
Persons registered as Order Processing Assistant Representative, UK/Canada Securities Representative, Options Representative, Corporate Securities Representative, Government Securities Representative, or Foreign Associate on/within two years before October 1, 2018 may maintain those registrations but cannot re-register if terminated for two or more years — Order Processing Assistants may not solicit, advise, execute, or accept municipal/DPP orders, must be hourly/salaried (no transaction-based compensation), must work under direct principal supervision, and may upgrade to other categories (terminating the OPA registration); Foreign Associates must be non-U.S. citizens/residents conducting all securities activities outside U.S. jurisdiction.
